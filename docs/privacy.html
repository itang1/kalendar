Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 3, 2026

Kalendar is designed to work entirely on your device and your own iCloud account. It is built so that your use of the app is nobody's business but your own.

What we collect

Nothing. Kalendar does not collect, transmit, or store any personal information on any server we control. It contains no analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind, and makes no network requests other than the iCloud syncing described below.

Your notes

Any notes you add to a day are stored on your device and synced through Apple's iCloud key-value storage, using your own iCloud account, so they appear on your other devices signed into the same Apple ID. They are never sent to us or to any other service. If you uninstall the app from one device, your notes remain in iCloud and are restored if you reinstall or open the app on another device signed into the same account.

Third parties

Kalendar uses no third-party services, SDKs, or frameworks that collect data.

Children

Because the app collects no data at all, it is safe for users of any age.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted on this page.
